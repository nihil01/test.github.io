Tours and attractions :

Gobustan

Discover diverse attractions across the Absheron peninsula on this full-day tour from Baku, with round-trip transfer from your hotel ensuring easy access to several off-road sites. Get up close to mud volcanoes in the Gobustan National Park, explore the Bibi Heybat Mosque and Ateshgah Temple, and see the Yanar Dag flames first-hand.

Rating: 10/10

Maiden Tower

The tower is covered by a cloud of mysteries and legends that are part of the national culture.

Rating: 10/10

National Carpet Museum

Opened in 2014, museum housing the cultural heritage of Azerbaijan's carpets from a hundred years ago to today. Building is shaped like a rolled up carpet with windows on the ends. Inside allows viewing of regional carpets through the ages - each level represents a different epoch, and explanation as to cultural design significance,colors,techniques.

Rating: 8/10

Fortress Walls of Icheri Sheher

Situated as a time capsul in the middle of the modern Baku, the old town is a must when visiting Azerbaijan.

Rating: 10/10

Martyrs' Lane

The monument is complete with a long alley of prominent soldiers as well as a tower with an everlasting flame.

Rating: 9/10

Baku Boulevard

"Bakı Bulvarı" is the green lung of Baku seafront. It was created at the beginning of the 20th c. Forming a contrast to the nearby cityscapes ruined by the oil industry, the boulevard offers the visitor many attractions. There are the Baku Ferris wheel, the Crystal Palace, an open air cinema, the impressive Carpet Museum and many other precious museums.

Rating: 8/10

Heydar Aliyev Cultural Center

The architecture is just spectacular. If you’re a museum & art enthusiast, then you’d probably require more than half a day to explore this beauty. The interiors are a flow of the exterior displaying the country’s rich culture in music, clothes, paintings, dolls, carpets etc along with another section dedicated to the namesake.

Rating: 10/10
